This generates a screening-level TIS designed to get your project through early review. Signal volumes are deterministically generated — for final design, replace them with measured turning-movement counts. Trip distribution uses distance-decay modeling; for a final TIS, you'd substitute an O-D matrix. The built-in limitations appendix makes this disclosure explicit so there's no ambiguity about scope.
Every report cites HCM 6th Edition for capacity and delay analysis, ITE Trip Generation 11th Edition for trip rates, MUTCD for signal warrants, AASHTO Green Book for geometric design, GDOT RPM for right-of-way approval procedures, and NCHRP 765 for screening-level trip distribution. All citations are inline so the reviewing PE can verify each figure.
The system includes 8+ pre-configured templates covering mid-rise apartments, suburban subdivisions, general office, medical office, retail shopping centers, restaurants, fast-casual dining, and hotels. Each template comes with ITE rates, default pass-by credits, and internal capture percentages that you can override for your specific project.
The optional sensitivity analysis runs 100 iterations with ±10% perturbation on trip generation rates and ±15% on existing intersection volumes. Results are reported at the P10, P50, and P90 levels, giving you a confidence band of ±15% on all delay-delta projections at an 80% confidence level. This strengthens the defensibility of your screening conclusions.
